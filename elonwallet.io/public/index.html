FAQ

How does the alpha work?
‍
All you have to do is register with a valid email address. Then a wallet will be automatically created for you. Currently we support managing your cryptocurrencies on Ethereum, Ethereum Goerli, Ethereum Sepolia, Polygon, Polygon Mumbai, Avalance C-Chain, Fantom Opera, Arbitrum One or the Binance Smart Chain.
‍
What is hardware-graded security?
‍
Hardware-graded security provides an additional layer of protection that applications can rely on. ElonWallet is built on Intel SGX, which ensures that your wallet keys remain securely encrypted even while in use. Only the CPU itself has access to the symmetric key needed to operate on the data, and keeps it in a secure location directly in the silicon to protect it from malicious entities or malware.
‍
Why not use software-based encryption?
‍
Data must be decrypted for a very short time during processing. Encryption in software cannot prevent others from accessing the data during the short time the data is unencrypted in memory. Adversaries could exploit this window to take over your wallets. In ElonWallet, we use hardware to protect the key material, which makes compromise much harder.
‍
What is confidential computing/enclave technology?
‍
An enclave is an isolated application running on an untrusted system. The application itself is fully encrypted and integrity protected by the CPU. Since the CPU is the only one with access to the encryption key, anyone else looking at the process memory will only see ciphertext. The technology is very fast and has little impact on application performance.
‍
How is my wallet key being stored?
‍
A big problem that arises when using crypto wallets is where and how the and how the wallet key is stored. In ElonWallet we do not want to have access to your keys. Therefore, we store your keys only in encrypted form and only allow your wallet service, protected by Intel SGX, to decrypt the keys when they are needed.
‍
What is passwordless authentication (WebAuthn)?
‍
With WebAuthn, your system authenticator or specialized roaming authenticator creates a unique key pair that is used only to authenticate to ElonWallet. During the authentication process, your authenticator provides proof that it has a secret key without ever revealing it to us. In addition to being easier to use, the process is much more secure than passwords. No more weak passwords or account compromises due to weak or phished passwords!
‍
What are Passkeys?
‍
Passkeys are the result of a joint effort by major technology companies such as Apple, Google, and Microsoft. Some time ago, WebAuthn credentials could only be used on one device. Passkeys extended the functionality by being syncable with other devices. For example, you can create a credential on your iPhone and then use the same credential on macOS through the ICloud keychain.
‍
Is my biometric data being sent to your servers?
‍
No, the biometric data stays on your system. The biometric verification is only done locally on your system to prove to your authenticator that it is really you who wants to use the WebAuthn credential to log in to ElonWallet.
‍
Do ElonWallet or the cloud providers have access to my wallet?
‍
No, your keys are stored encrypted by the CPU. Because of confidential computing, your wallet keys remain confidential, even when sending transactions. Before a transaction is sent from your wallet, a WebAuthn authentication ceremony is required to authorize it. Since only you can authorize it, no one else can make transactions.
‍
How can I be sure ElonWallet doesn't have a backdoor in its system?
‍
We are an open source project and all our code is available on GitHub. Anyone who knows GoLang and Nuxt can verify our code. Intel SGX has a feature called remote attestation. With remote attestation, you can verify for yourself that our published code on GitHub is actually running in the enclave.
‍
Why should I trust ElonWallet?
‍
The short answer is you don't. Everything we do is transparent and publicly verifiable. All you have to do is trust the technology we use (Intel SGX, WebAuthn).
‍
What is a cold wallet?
‍
A cold wallet is a type of hardware module that isolates your wallet keys from the operating system. Even if your device is completely compromised, the keys in the hardware module remain inaccessible to the attacker.
‍
What is a non-custodial wallet?
‍
It is a wallet where you are the only person with access to your private keys.
‍
How can I use ElonWallet cross-device?
‍
Accessing ElonWallet on a new device requires either syncing a previously registered credential to the new device, using a roaming authenticator, or registering an additional credential on the new device by authenticating using the one-time password feature.
